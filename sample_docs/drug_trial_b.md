# Phase II trial of betapril in heart failure with preserved ejection fraction

- Trial ID: SAMPLE-HFPEF-004
- Status: Example document for SLIDERS. Not a real study.

## Abstract

**Background.** Betapril is a novel small-molecule natriuretic peptide potentiator being developed for heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF), a condition with limited pharmacological options.

**Methods.** We conducted a Phase II, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled dose-finding trial at 18 academic medical centers. Patients with symptomatic HFpEF (LVEF ≥ 50%, NYHA class II-III, elevated NT-proBNP) were randomized 1:1:1:1 to betapril 40 mg, betapril 80 mg, betapril 160 mg, or placebo, all once daily for 24 weeks. The primary endpoint was change from baseline in NT-proBNP at week 24.

**Results.** We randomized 284 participants (mean age 68.2 years, 54% female, median baseline NT-proBNP 912 pg/mL). At week 24, the geometric mean ratio of NT-proBNP versus placebo was 0.92 (95% CI 0.83-1.02) for the 40 mg arm, 0.81 (0.72-0.91) for the 80 mg arm, and 0.78 (0.70-0.88) for the 160 mg arm. KCCQ-OSS improved by 4.1, 6.8, and 7.2 points respectively, versus 2.0 in the placebo arm. Treatment-emergent adverse events were similar across arms; hypotension occurred in 3% of the 160 mg arm and 1% of placebo.

**Conclusions.** Betapril produced a dose-dependent reduction in NT-proBNP and improvement in quality of life in HFpEF over 24 weeks. The 80 mg dose offered a favorable balance of efficacy and tolerability and is being carried forward into a Phase III outcomes trial.

## Methods

### Design
Phase II, multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group trial with four arms. The trial was registered prior to enrollment and conducted under Good Clinical Practice guidelines.

### Participants
Key inclusion criteria: age ≥ 50 years; LVEF ≥ 50% on qualifying echocardiogram; NYHA class II or III symptoms; NT-proBNP ≥ 300 pg/mL (≥ 600 pg/mL if atrial fibrillation present); stable background HFpEF therapy for at least 4 weeks. Key exclusions: eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m²; systolic BP < 100 mmHg; prior intolerance to natriuretic-peptide-potentiating agents.

### Interventions
Participants received oral betapril at 40 mg, 80 mg, 160 mg, or matching placebo, once daily for 24 weeks. Background HFpEF therapy (diuretics, SGLT2 inhibitors, mineralocorticoid receptor antagonists) was continued at the discretion of the investigator.

### Outcomes
The primary endpoint was the ratio of NT-proBNP at week 24 compared to baseline, expressed as a geometric mean ratio versus placebo. Secondary endpoints included change in KCCQ Overall Summary Score (KCCQ-OSS), 6-minute walk distance, and safety.

## Results

### Enrollment
Between October 2023 and June 2024, 410 patients were screened and 284 randomized: 70 to betapril 40 mg, 72 to betapril 80 mg, 71 to betapril 160 mg, and 71 to placebo. Baseline characteristics were balanced across arms. Mean age was 68.2 years; 54% were female; 38% had atrial fibrillation; 47% had diabetes.

### Primary outcome
The geometric mean ratio of NT-proBNP at week 24 versus placebo was:
- Betapril 40 mg: 0.92 (95% CI 0.83-1.02)
- Betapril 80 mg: 0.81 (0.72-0.91)
- Betapril 160 mg: 0.78 (0.70-0.88)
Test for trend across dose P < 0.001.

### Secondary outcomes
KCCQ-OSS improved from baseline by 2.0 (placebo), 4.1 (40 mg), 6.8 (80 mg), and 7.2 (160 mg) points. 6-minute walk distance improved by 8, 18, 32, and 30 m across the four arms.

### Safety
Treatment-emergent adverse events occurred in 40-48% of participants across arms with no significant between-group difference. Clinically meaningful hypotension (systolic BP < 90 mmHg requiring intervention) occurred in 1% (placebo), 1% (40 mg), 3% (80 mg), and 3% (160 mg). No drug-related serious adverse events were reported.

## Discussion

In this Phase II dose-finding study, betapril produced a dose-dependent reduction in NT-proBNP and an improvement in patient-reported quality of life in HFpEF. The 80 mg dose achieved near-maximal efficacy on the primary endpoint and carried a lower burden of hypotension than the 160 mg dose, making it the preferred dose for subsequent Phase III evaluation.

Limitations include the modest sample size, the 24-week duration which does not permit assessment of hospitalization or mortality endpoints, and enrollment predominantly from academic centers.

## Conclusion

Betapril demonstrated a dose-dependent NT-proBNP reduction and quality-of-life improvement in HFpEF. The 80 mg once-daily dose is being advanced into a Phase III trial powered on cardiovascular death and heart-failure hospitalization.
